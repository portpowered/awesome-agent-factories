# Code of Conduct

This code of conduct applies to everyone participating in **Awesome AI Agent Factories** community spaces, including GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other project channels linked from this repository.

Our goal is a respectful, factual environment where contributors can improve a curated list about managing groups of AI agents and their flows.

## Scope

This policy covers:

- Interactions in this repository's issues and pull requests
- Repository discussions and comments on proposed list changes
- Conduct directed at maintainers, contributors, or other participants in project spaces

It does not govern third-party projects linked from the list. Report security or abuse concerns about linked resources under [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) when that policy is available.

## Standards of behavior

Participants are expected to:

- Be respectful and constructive, especially when disagreeing about scope or categorization
- Stay factual in reviews and comments; focus on the resource, formatting, and list conventions
- Accept good-faith feedback from maintainers and other contributors
- Credit others' work and avoid misrepresenting resources or policies
- Respect differing experience levels with multi-agent systems and agent-factory concepts

## Unacceptable behavior

The following are not tolerated:

- Harassment, threats, intimidation, or sustained personal attacks
- Discriminatory, derogatory, or exclusionary language or imagery
- Sexualized attention or unwelcome advances
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Deliberate disruption, trolling, spam, or bad-faith arguments
- Retaliation against someone for reporting a conduct concern in good faith

Marketing hype, duplicate submissions, and out-of-scope resources are handled under [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md); repeated bad-faith submissions after maintainer feedback may also be treated as conduct issues.

## Enforcement responsibilities

Repository maintainers are responsible for reviewing reported conduct concerns and deciding appropriate responses.

Enforcement is **manual**. There is no dedicated moderation team, automated conduct bot, or 24/7 coverage. Maintainers will respond as they are able.

Maintainers may:

- Request edits or clarification in issues or pull requests
- Temporarily or permanently restrict participation in repository spaces
- Close or lock threads that violate this policy
- Reject or revert contributions that violate conduct expectations

Maintainers who participate in discussions are expected to follow this code of conduct and to recuse themselves from enforcement decisions where they have a direct conflict of interest.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it through one of these channels:

1. **GitHub** — Contact a repository maintainer directly:
   - [@portpowered](https://github.com/portpowered)
   - [@AndreasAbdi](https://github.com/AndreasAbdi)
2. **Private report** — Use [GitHub private vulnerability reporting](https://github.com/portpowered/awesome-agent-factories/security/advisories/new) for sensitive conduct reports if you cannot use a public issue safely.

Maintainer contact details and review policy will also be listed in [MAINTAINERS.md](MAINTAINERS.md) when that document is published.

Include:

- What happened and where (issue, pull request, or discussion link)
- Who was involved
- Why the behavior violates this policy
- Whether you prefer a private response

Reports are reviewed by maintainers in good faith. We aim to acknowledge reports within a reasonable time, but response times depend on maintainer availability.

## Consequences

Maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate, including:

- A private warning
- A public request to stop the behavior
- Requiring an apology or correction before further participation
- Blocking further interaction in repository spaces
- Escalation to GitHub support for severe or repeated violations

Consequences are intended to protect participants and keep collaboration productive, not to punish good-faith mistakes.

## Attribution

This policy is adapted from common open-source community guidelines. It is tailored for a small, manually maintained awesome list and may be updated as maintainer policy matures.
